## Short Project Pitch

I built a retail operations analytics project that simulates an outdoor apparel business similar to a The North Face-style retailer. The dataset is at the store-date-product level, so I could analyze revenue, units sold, customer visits, transactions, promotions, inventory levels, conversion rate, and sell-through rate. The main business finding was that premium Jackets drove the most revenue, but fast-moving lower-price items such as accessories and base layers created repeated low-stock risk. I used the analysis to recommend better replenishment planning, targeted promotions, and staffing adjustments based on customer traffic and conversion.

## Interview Talking Points

### Why this project?

I wanted a project that felt close to real retail operations work. Instead of only looking at sales totals, I included inventory, traffic, transactions, promotions, and store-level detail so the analysis could support merchandising, operations, marketing, and finance decisions.

### Why simulated data?

Real The North Face store-level sales and inventory data is not publicly available. I clearly labeled the dataset as simulated and designed it to be realistic enough for portfolio demonstration without pretending it is real company data.

### What business problem did you solve?

The project identifies which products drive revenue, which products are at risk of low stock, which products may need targeted promotion, and how store traffic and conversion can support staffing decisions.

### What metrics did you use?

I used total revenue, units sold, average order value, conversion rate, sell-through rate, low-stock days, revenue by category, revenue by month, top products by revenue, and store-level performance.

### What was the most important insight?

The biggest revenue driver was Jackets, especially Alpine Down Parka, but the biggest inventory risk appeared in smaller fast-moving items such as Summit Beanie, Trail Gloves, Insulated Bottle, and Thermal Crew Base Layer. That means operations teams should not only focus on high-ticket products; they also need to protect availability for high-velocity add-on products.

### What would you improve with real company data?

With real data, I would add product margin, discount depth, stockout records, return rates, store labor hours, weather data, and marketing campaign spend. That would allow deeper analysis of profitability, promotion ROI, staffing efficiency, and lost sales.

### How does this relate to internship roles?

- For business analyst roles, it shows KPI design, data cleaning, analysis, and recommendations.
- For retail operations roles, it shows inventory monitoring, store performance, staffing implications, and stockout risk.
- For merchandising roles, it shows product ranking, sell-through, slow-moving products, and replenishment decisions.
- For finance roles, it shows revenue concentration, AOV, and category performance.
- For marketing roles, it shows promotion analysis opportunities and conversion improvement.
